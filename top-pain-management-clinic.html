What is a Pain Management Clinic?

A Pain Management Clinic is defined as any hospital or facility that practices specialized medical treatment and provides diagnosis of certain types of pain. These clinics serve patients suffering from such chronic pain conditions as arthritis, back pain, migraine headache, neuropathy, and post-surgical pain, among others. The purpose of this clinic is not just to alleviate pain but also to address the basic problem for long-term, sustainable solutions.

Pain management clinics treat common conditions.

  • Neck pain - Can be caused by muscle strain, disc problems, or poor posture.
  • Trigeminal Neuralgia - Is a chronic pain disorder affecting the trigeminal nerve, resulting in severe facial pain.
  • Back pain - Pain can be both chronic and acute, resulting from a spinal condition, injury, or ruptured disc.
  • Knee Pain- Pain caused by osteoarthritis, ligament injuries, or meniscus tears.
  • Buttock Pain- Sciatica, muscle tightness, or pain due to pelvic conditions.
  • Shoulder Pain- Pain caused by rotator cuff injury, frozen shoulder, or arthritis.
  • Vascular Diseases- Pain associated with diseases such as peripheral artery disease and varicose veins.
  • Hip Pain- Pain due to arthritis, bursitis, or fractures, affecting functions and mobility.
  • Cancer Pain and Palliative Care - Pain relief aimed at maintaining comfort and enhancing the quality of life of cancer patients.
